The article reports that involuntary hunger among Filipino families decreased to 16.1% in the second quarter of 2025, based on the latest SWS survey. The national drop was mainly driven by a large decline in hunger cases in Mindanao, although Metro Manila and the Visayas experienced slight increases. The report also highlights changes in moderate and severe hunger levels across regions. Additionally, hunger rates fell among both self-rated poor and non-poor households, showing overall improvement despite uneven results nationwide.

How can community help?

Communities can help reduce hunger by organizing local food drives, community pantries, and feeding programs that provide immediate support to families in need. They can also promote urban gardening and backyard farming so households can grow their own food and lessen expenses. Local leaders and volunteers can work together to connect people to livelihood programs and skills training that help them earn more and become food-secure. Schools and barangays can raise awareness about proper nutrition and government assistance programs. By working together, communities can strengthen support systems and reduce the number of families experiencing hunger.
